## Output Template: Jira Ticket

You MUST use EXACTLY the sections below — no more, no less. Do not invent additional sections such as "Sources to investigate", "Additional context", "Output", "Due", or anything else. Do not attribute knowledge or facts to individual team members. Present all information as factual observations.

Format each section with the bold label on its own line, followed by the content on the next line(s).

**Title**
Short descriptive title for the ticket. Be direct and capture the core need.

**Description**
One to three sentences describing the need or problem being solved. Focus on what and why, not how.

**Current State**
One to three sentences describing the current situation or problem. Focus on what exists today and why it needs to change.

**Technical Notes**
Bulleted list of relevant technical context, constraints, or background from the meeting. Include references to specific software, libraries, or applications mentioned in context. Be concise, do not over explain.

**Acceptance Criteria**
A numbered list of goal-oriented criteria. Each item describes an observable outcome when the ticket is complete. Do not include implementation steps, technical instructions, or "how to" language. Do not include any coding samples. This section is mandatory.
